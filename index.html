Welcome to People and Geography Quiz from Harvard University

Please read the following information about this research and indicate that you agree to participate.

Purpose of the research: To understand how well people can place groups in geographic space and how this relates to their attitudes about groups.

What you will do in this research: If you decide to participate, you will complete one survey in which you will be asked to try to place groups on a map.

Time required: The survey will take approximately 5 minutes to complete.

Benefits: There are no direct benefits, but you may find it interesting.

Confidentiality: Your responses will be kept confidential. When research results are reported, responses will be aggregated (added together) and described in summary.

Questions or concerns: Please contact Professor Ryan Enos: renos@gov.harvard.edu or 617-496-1724.

Participation and withdrawal: Your participation is completely voluntary, and you may quit at any time without penalty. You may also skip any question, but continue to complete the rest of the survey.

This is the zipcode you entered. Each outlined shape is a neighborhood in the zipcode. Do you know where to find different groups of people in this zipcode?

On the next screen, you will be asked to indicate in which neighborhood do most people from certain groups live.

For example, first you will be asked to indicate in which neighborhood do most Asian people live. Click on a neighborhood to indicate where you think most Asian people live. The neighborhood will turn orange to indicate your choice. You will then be asked to do the same thing for three other groups.

You can choose the same neighborhood for more than one group. You can also choose “Skip Group” if you don’t know.

When you’ve made all your selections, click “Save,” and we will show you the correct answers.

You can also change the basemap with the button in the upper right corner of the screen. And zoom in and out with the buttons on the left.
